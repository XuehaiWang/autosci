## Procedure

1. **Define scope**
   - Clarify the research question and key terms
   - Set inclusion/exclusion criteria (date range, venue quality, relevance)
   - Identify 3-5 primary search queries

2. **Search**
   - Use multiple sources: Google Scholar, ArXiv, Semantic Scholar
   - Try both broad and specific queries
   - Check reference lists of key papers (snowball sampling)
   - Aim for 30-50 candidate papers initially

3. **Screen**
   - Read titles and abstracts to filter by relevance
   - Keep 10-20 key papers for detailed reading
   - Prioritize: recent survey papers, highly-cited works, and directly relevant studies

4. **Extract**
   - For each paper, note:
     - Method/approach
     - Dataset(s) used
     - Key results (with numbers)
     - Limitations acknowledged
     - How it relates to our research question

5. **Synthesize**
   - Identify common themes across papers
   - Note contradictions and open debates
   - Map the research landscape: what's solved, what's open
   - Identify gaps our work could address

6. **Output**
   - Produce a structured summary with:
     - Key findings table (paper | method | dataset | result)
     - Research gaps and opportunities
     - Recommended next steps
   - Save to a file for future reference

## Tips

- Start broad, then narrow — don't over-filter initially
- Always check citation count AND recency
- Note conflicting results explicitly — they often indicate interesting research directions
- Look for survey/review papers first — they save time
- If a subfield is unfamiliar, read one survey before diving into individual papers
